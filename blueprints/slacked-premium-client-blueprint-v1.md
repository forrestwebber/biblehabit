## 1. Engagement Overview

Jack Decker runs a personal injury law firm in Austin, TX. His goal is to increase qualified leads from potential clients who've been in car accidents, workplace injuries, or slip-and-fall incidents in the Austin metro area. He currently gets most referrals through word-of-mouth and wants to build a digital presence that generates 15–25 inbound leads per month within 6 months.

**What Jack is paying for:**
- A full-service digital marketing operation he never has to manage
- Measurable growth in website traffic, search rankings, and qualified leads
- A dedicated team that communicates progress weekly and proves ROI monthly
- Professional web presence, content, social media, email marketing, and outreach

**Our commitment:** Jack should feel like he hired a full in-house marketing department — but all he sees is clean deliverables, clear updates, and results.

## 2. Phase 1: Onboarding (Week 1)

### Day 1: Discovery Call

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Scheduling** | Calendly link sent within 1 hour of signed contract |

**Discovery Call Agenda:**
1. Jack's story — why he started, what sets him apart (brand voice extraction)
2. Current client acquisition channels and cost-per-case
3. Practice areas ranked by revenue and priority
4. Geographic targets (Austin, Round Rock, Cedar Park, etc.)
5. Ideal client profile (demographics, case types, urgency triggers)
6. Existing assets audit (website, social accounts, Google Business Profile, reviews)
7. Competitors Jack respects or fears
8. Timeline expectations and definition of success
9. Communication preferences (how often, what format, what time zone)

## 3. Phase 2: Monthly Deliverables ($5,000 Tier)

### 3.1 SEO Blog Content — 8 Posts/Month

| Attribute | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| **Volume** | 8 articles per month |
| **Word count** | 1,200–2,000 words each |
| **Keyword targeting** | Local + practice-area long-tail keywords |
| **On-page SEO** | Title tags, meta descriptions, H2/H3 structure, internal links, schema markup |
| **Turnaround** | Drafts posted to BC for review → published within 48 hrs of approval |
| **Publishing** | WordPress/Vercel deploy + GitHub backup |

**Sample Keyword Targets:**
- "Austin personal injury lawyer"
- "car accident attorney Austin TX"
- "slip and fall lawyer near me Austin"
- "what to do after a car accident in Texas"
- "how much is my personal injury case worth"
- "Austin workplace injury attorney"
- "best personal injury lawyer in Austin reviews"
- "Texas statute of limitations personal injury"

**Content Process:**
1. Keyword research and topic approval (internal)
2. AI-assisted first draft (internal)
3. Human editorial review and legal accuracy check (internal)
4. Draft posted to Basecamp for Jack's review (client-visible)
5. Jack approves or requests changes
6. Published to website, indexed, shared on social

### 3.2 Social Media — 20 Posts/Month

| Platform | Posts/Month | Content Types |
|----------|------------|---------------|
| Facebook | 8 | Case tips, testimonials, firm updates, community posts |
| Instagram | 6 | Branded graphics, Reels, behind-the-scenes, team spotlights |
| LinkedIn | 4 | Thought leadership, case law updates, professional insights |
| Google Business Profile | 2 | Posts with CTAs, offers, event announcements |

**Content Calendar:**
- Delivered on the 25th of the prior month for next-month approval
- Posted to Basecamp as a visual calendar (client-visible)
- Jack can request changes, add ideas, or veto any post
- All posts scheduled via Buffer/Later with tracking links

### 3.3 Google Business Profile Optimization

**Monthly GBP Tasks:**
- Respond to all new reviews within 24 hours (personalized, not templated)
- Post 2 GBP updates per month (events, offers, tips)
- Update business info as needed (hours, services, photos)
- Add 5+ new photos per month (office, team, community events)
- Monitor and dispute any spam reviews
- Q&A section management
- Track local pack rankings weekly

### 3.4 Monthly Analytics Report

**Delivered by the 5th of each month. Posted to Basecamp (client-visible).**

**Report Sections:**
1. **Executive Summary** — 3-sentence overview of the month (wins + areas of focus)
2. **Website Traffic** — Sessions, users, pageviews, bounce rate, time on site (vs. prior month + vs. baseline)
3. **SEO Rankings** — Keyword position changes for top 20 target keywords
4. **Lead Generation** — Form submissions, phone calls (tracked via CallRail/WhatConverts), live chat inquiries
5. **Content Performance** — Top-performing blog posts, social posts, email campaigns
6. **Google Business Profile** — Views, clicks, calls, direction requests, review count + avg rating
7. **Social Media** — Follower growth, engagement rate, top posts
8. **Email Marketing** — Open rates, click rates, subscriber growth
9. **Outreach** — Prospects contacted, responses, meetings booked
10. **Recommendations** — What we'll adjust next month and why

**Report Format:**
- Clean PDF with branded header
- Plain language — no unexplained jargon
- Every metric includes context: "This means..." or "This matters because..."
- Comparison charts: this month vs. last month vs. baseline

| Task | Frequency |

| New pages (practice area, landing pages) | As strategy requires |

### 3.6 Email Marketing — 2 Campaigns/Month

| Campaign Type | Frequency | Details |
|--------------|-----------|---------|
| Newsletter | 1x/month | Legal tips, firm news, recent blog posts, CTA to schedule consultation |
| Nurture/Drip | 1x/month | Targeted to leads who haven't converted — case studies, FAQ, social proof |

**Email Platform:** Mailchimp or ConvertKit
**List Building:** Website opt-in forms, consultation follow-ups, downloadable guides (e.g., "What to Do After a Car Accident in Austin — Free Guide")

### 3.7 Calendly Setup for Client Intake

- Custom Calendly booking page branded to Decker Law
- Intake form embedded: name, phone, email, case type, brief description
- Confirmation emails with firm branding and what to expect
- Calendar synced with Jack's calendar (Google/Outlook)
- Automated reminders: 24 hours and 1 hour before appointment
- No-show follow-up sequence (automated email + text)
- Booking link embedded on website, GBP, social bios, and email signatures

### 3.8 Lead Tracking Dashboard

**Dashboard Components (client-visible, real-time):**
- Total leads this month (by source: organic, paid, referral, social, direct)
- Lead status pipeline: New → Contacted → Consultation Booked → Retained → Closed
- Conversion rates at each stage
- Cost per lead by channel
- Lead response time (target: under 5 minutes for form submissions)
- Monthly trend charts

**Technical Setup:**
- Supabase backend (tables: clients, deliverables, leads, analytics)
- Client-facing dashboard at `dash.slacked.co/decker-law` or embedded in Basecamp
- Updated daily via automated sync

### 3.9 Outreach — 50 Prospects/Month

**Target Prospects:**
- Local businesses and professionals who refer PI cases (chiropractors, auto body shops, ER doctors, insurance agents)
- Community organizations and event sponsors
- Local media contacts for PR opportunities

**Outreach Process:**
1. Research and build prospect list (50/month)
2. Personalized email outreach (not mass blast)
3. Follow-up sequence: Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7
4. Track responses, meetings booked, referral partnerships formed
5. Monthly report: contacted, responded, converted

### 3.10 Conversion Tracking

**What We Track:**
- Website visitor → form submission (conversion rate)
- Form submission → consultation booked (booking rate)
- Consultation → retained client (close rate)
- Retained client → case value (revenue attribution)
- Full funnel: ad spend / marketing cost → cases signed → revenue generated → ROI

**Tools:**
- Google Analytics 4 with conversion events
- CallRail for phone tracking with call recording
- UTM parameters on all links
- CRM integration (if Jack uses one) or our Supabase lead tracker
- Monthly ROI calculation in analytics report
